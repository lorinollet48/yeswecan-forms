Main
Pan-Seared Salmon with Lemon Herb Crust
Serves 220 min
Smoked paprika gives the crust color, dill and thyme bridge the brine flavors, and the lemon peel ties it to a Blood Orange Marmalade glaze that finishes the whole dish.
Ingredients
  • Friday Fish Blend1½ tsp
  • Salmon fillets, skin-on2 × 6 oz
  • Olive oil1 tbsp
  • Blood Orange Marmalade2 tbsp
  • Butter1 tbsp
  • Lemon wedgesto serve
Method
  1. Pat salmon dry. Press Friday Fish Blend onto flesh side.
  2. Heat olive oil in cast iron over medium-high. Add salmon skin-side up. Cook 3–4 min until golden.
  3. Flip. Add butter and Blood Orange Marmalade. Spoon over salmon as it melts. Cook 2–3 more min for medium.
  4. Rest 2 min. Serve with pan glaze and lemon.

Main · Low & Slow
Low & Slow Smoked Brisket
Serves 8–108–12 hrs
Coarse black pepper and smoked paprika form the bark; dry mustard adds the back-heat Texas pitmasters depend on. Dr. Pepper BBQ Sauce in the final hour gives a lacquered finish.
Ingredients
  • Chuckwagon Brisket Blend3 tbsp
  • Beef brisket, full flat8–10 lbs
  • Yellow mustard (binder)2 tbsp
  • Dr. Pepper BBQ Sauce½ cup
  • Bread & Butter Picklesalongside
Method
  1. Apply mustard as binder. Press Chuckwagon blend on all surfaces. Rest overnight.
  2. Smoke fat-side up at 225°F to 165°F internal (~5–6 hrs).
  3. Wrap in butcher paper. Continue to 203–205°F (~3–4 more hrs).
  4. Rest 1 hr. Brush Dr. Pepper BBQ Sauce. Return 15 min to set glaze. Slice against the grain.

Chuckwagon Smash Burgers
Serves 420 min
The blend's coarse pepper and paprika form a phenomenal crust. Bread & Butter Pickles are non-negotiable. Dr. Pepper BBQ Sauce mixed into the burger sauce makes the whole thing.
Ingredients

  • Ground beef 80/201½ lbs
  • Bread & Butter Pickles8–12 slices
  • Dr. Pepper BBQ Sauce + mayo2 tbsp each
  • American cheese4 slices
  • Brioche buns, toasted4
Method

Main
Five-Spice Duck Breast
Serves 245 min
Score the skin, render it slowly, let the five-spice bloom in the fat. Hot Pepper Jelly as a finishing drizzle bridges sweet and heat in exactly the right way.
Ingredients
  • Hunter's Cabin Duck Blend1½ tsp
  • Duck breasts, skin-on2
  • Hot Pepper Jelly1 tbsp
  • Flaky sea saltpinch
Method
  1. Score skin in crosshatch. Rub Duck Blend on flesh side and lightly on skin. Rest 30 min at room temp.
  2. Place skin-side down in a cold pan. Bring slowly to medium heat to render fat and build bark.
  3. Cook skin-down 8–10 min until deep golden. Flip, cook 3–4 min for medium-rare (130°F).
  4. Rest 5 min. Slice thin. Drizzle Hot Pepper Jelly over. Finish with flaky salt.

Method
  1. Choose your fruit: cranberries + orange zest · strawberries · peaches · apple + cinnamon.

Honey & Vine Old Fashioned
Serves 15 min
The brine replaces the sugar cube — NH honey carries the sweetness and floral complexity that simple syrup can't. ACV lifts the bourbon the way bitters do.
Ingredients
  • Honey & Vine Brine2 tbsp
  • Bourbon2 oz
  • Aromatic bitters2 dashes
  • Large ice cube1
  • Orange peel — express and drop1 strip
Method
  1. Add Honey & Vine Brine and bitters to a mixing glass with ice. Stir 10 seconds.
  2. Add bourbon. Stir 15 more seconds.
  3. Strain over a large ice cube in a rocks glass.
  4. Express orange peel over the surface, run around the rim, drop in.

Honey & Vine Mustard Glaze
Makes ¼ cup10 min
Honey & Vine Brine and Spiced Whole Grain Mustard make a glaze for salmon, chicken, or roasted carrots — and a board dipping sauce you'll reach for every time.
Ingredients
  • Honey & Vine Brine3 tbsp
  • Spiced Whole Grain Mustard1 tbsp
  • Butter (for pan sauce version)1 tbsp
  • Protein or vegetables of choiceas needed
Method
  1. Whisk Honey & Vine Brine and Spiced Whole Grain Mustard together.
  2. As a glaze: brush over protein in the final 5 min of cooking.
  3. As a pan sauce: deglaze after searing, add butter and swirl to emulsify.
  4. As a board drizzle: serve as-is in a ramekin alongside cheese and bread.
