Main · 20 minutes
Pan-Seared Salmon with Lemon Herb Crust
Serves 220 min
The Friday Fish Blend was built for this — smoked paprika gives the crust color, dill and thyme bridge to the brine flavors in the lineup, and the lemon peel ties it to a Blood Orange Marmalade glaze that finishes the whole dish.
Ingredients
  • Friday Fish Blend1½ tsp
  • Salmon fillets, skin-on2 × 6 oz
  • Olive oil1 tbsp
  • Blood Orange Marmalade2 tbsp
  • Butter1 tbsp
  • Lemon wedgesto serve
Method
  1. Pat salmon dry. Press Friday Fish Blend onto flesh side, pressing to adhere.
  2. Heat olive oil in a cast iron skillet over medium-high until shimmering. Add salmon skin-side up. Cook 3–4 minutes until golden crust forms.
  3. Flip. Add butter and Blood Orange Marmalade to pan. Spoon over salmon as it melts. Cook 2–3 more minutes for medium.
  4. Rest 2 minutes. Serve with pan glaze spooned over and lemon alongside.

Spice Blend Chuckwagon Brisket Blend 4 oz · $14
Main · Low & Slow
Low & Slow Smoked Brisket
Serves 8–108–12 hrs
The Chuckwagon blend is built for this — coarse black pepper and smoked paprika form the bark, dry mustard adds the back-heat Texas pitmasters depend on. Mop with Dr. Pepper BBQ Sauce in the final hour for a lacquered finish.
Ingredients
  • Chuckwagon Brisket Blend3 tbsp
  • Beef brisket, full flat8–10 lbs
  • Yellow mustard (binder)2 tbsp
  • Dr. Pepper BBQ Sauce — mop½ cup
  • Bread & Butter Pickles — servealongside
Method
  1. Apply thin mustard coat to brisket as binder. Press Chuckwagon blend on all surfaces. Rest overnight in refrigerator.
  2. Smoke fat-side up at 225°F over oak or hickory. Internal temp 165°F (~5–6 hrs).
  3. Wrap in butcher paper. Continue smoking to 203–205°F (~3–4 more hrs).
  4. Rest 1 hour wrapped. Unwrap and brush Dr. Pepper BBQ Sauce. Return to smoker uncovered 15 min to set glaze.
  5. Slice against the grain. Serve with Bread & Butter Pickles and extra sauce.

Spice Blend Hunter's Cabin Duck Blend 2 oz · $10
Main · 45 minutes
Five-Spice Duck Breast
Serves 245 min
Classic French technique, Chinese-inspired spice, New Hampshire jar. Score the skin, render it slowly, let the five-spice bloom in the fat. Hot Pepper Jelly as a finishing drizzle bridges sweet and heat perfectly.
Ingredients
  • Hunter's Cabin Duck Blend1½ tsp
  • Duck breasts, skin-on2
  • Hot Pepper Jelly — finish1 tbsp
  • Flaky sea saltpinch
Method
  1. Score skin in crosshatch pattern — do not cut into meat. Rub Duck Blend on flesh side and lightly on skin. Rest 30 min at room temp.
  2. Place skin-side down in cold pan. Bring to medium heat slowly — this renders the fat and creates the bark rather than steaming.
  3. Cook skin-side down 8–10 min until deep golden. Tip off rendered fat as needed. Flip, cook 3–4 min for medium-rare (130°F).
  4. Rest 5 min. Slice thin. Drizzle Hot Pepper Jelly over slices. Finish with flaky salt.

Cocktail · 5 minutes
Christmas Pickle Bloody Mary
Serves 15 min
Pickle brine in a Bloody Mary is the standard. Christmas Pickle brine — with its warm spiced notes — elevates it to something genuinely seasonal. The spear as the garnish is the finishing touch.
Ingredients
  • Christmas Pickle brine2 tbsp
  • Vodka1.5 oz
  • Tomato juice4 oz
  • Lemon juice½ oz
  • Hot sauce, Worcestershiredash each
  • Christmas Pickle spear — garnish1
Method
  1. Combine pickle brine, vodka, tomato juice, lemon juice, hot sauce, and Worcestershire in a shaker with ice.
  2. Shake gently — don't over-dilute.
  3. Pour into a tall glass over fresh ice.
  4. Garnish with Christmas Pickle spear. The spice in the brine will continue seasoning the drink as you sip.

Dressing · 5 minutes
Salt & Vine Vinaigrette
Makes ½ cup5 min
Four ingredients in the brine — four ingredients in the dressing. The salt is already balanced, the acid is already there. Spiced Whole Grain Mustard as the emulsifier is the only addition that matters. Four ingredients in the brine, four in the dressing — it practically makes itself.
Ingredients
  • Salt & Vine Brine3 tbsp
  • Spiced Whole Grain Mustard1 tsp
  • Good olive oil¼ cup
  • Honey (optional)1 tsp
Method
  1. Combine brine and mustard in a small jar or bowl. Whisk or shake to combine.
  2. Stream in olive oil while whisking to emulsify. Add honey if you want balance.
  3. Taste — adjust with more brine for acid, more oil for richness.
  4. Use immediately on greens or store refrigerated up to 1 week. Shake before each use.

Salt & Vine Seasonal Shrubs
Makes 1 cup shrub5 min + 48 hrs
The brine is your base — you choose the fruit. Add cranberries for a holiday shrub, strawberries for spring, peaches for summer. The cocktail builds itself: 2 tbsp shrub, your spirit of choice, sparkling water.
Ingredients
  • Salt & Vine Brine1 cup
  • Fresh fruit of choice (see below)½ cup
  • Sparkling water — to serve4–5 oz
  • Spirits of choice — optional1.5 oz
Method
  1. Choose your fruit: cranberries + orange zest (holiday) · strawberries (spring) · peaches (summer) · apple + cinnamon (fall).
  2. Combine brine and fruit in a jar. Seal. Refrigerate 48 hours.
  3. Strain through a fine mesh sieve. Discard solids.
  4. Serve: 2 tbsp shrub over ice, top with sparkling water. Or add 1.5 oz gin, bourbon, or vodka. Garnish with fresh fruit.

Cocktail · 5 minutes
Honey & Vine Old Fashioned
Serves 15 min
The brine replaces the sugar cube — NH honey carries the sweetness and floral complexity that simple syrup can't. ACV lifts the bourbon the way bitters do. One jar does the work of three cocktail ingredients.
Ingredients
  • Honey & Vine Brine2 tbsp
  • Bourbon (2 oz)2 oz
  • Aromatic bitters2 dashes
  • Large ice cube1
  • Orange peel — express and drop1 strip
Method
  1. Add Honey & Vine Brine and bitters to a mixing glass with ice. Stir 10 seconds.
  2. Add bourbon. Stir 15 more seconds — you want dilution, just not too much.
  3. Strain over a large ice cube in a rocks glass.
  4. Express orange peel over the surface, run around the rim, drop in. Serve immediately.

Sauce / Glaze · 10 minutes
Honey & Vine Mustard Glaze
Makes ¼ cup10 min
Two Yes We Can! products, one sauce. Honey & Vine Brine and Spiced Whole Grain Mustard together make a glaze that works on salmon, chicken, roasted carrots, or as a dipping sauce alongside a board.
Ingredients
  • Honey & Vine Brine3 tbsp
  • Spiced Whole Grain Mustard1 tbsp
  • Butter (for pan sauce version)1 tbsp
  • Protein of choice — salmon, chicken, pork, carrotsas needed
Method
  1. Whisk Honey & Vine Brine and Spiced Whole Grain Mustard together.
  2. For a glaze: brush over protein in the final 5 minutes of cooking. The honey caramelizes, the mustard blooms.
  3. For a pan sauce: after searing protein, deglaze the pan with the mixture. Add butter and swirl to emulsify. Spoon over plated protein.
  4. For a board drizzle: serve as-is in a small ramekin for dipping cheese or bread.
